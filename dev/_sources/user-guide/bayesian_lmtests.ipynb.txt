    "# Bayesian Spatial Diagnostics\n",
    "\n",
    "This notebook is the canonical user guide for Bayesian Lagrange-Multiplier (LM)\n",
    "specification testing in `bayespecon`.  It covers\n",
    "\n",
    "1. the **inventory of LM tests** exposed by the package (cross-section, panel,\n",
    "   and spatial-flow families),\n",
    "2. **direct calls** to the test functions for cross-sectional models,\n",
    "3. the **method-based API** (`spatial_diagnostics()` and\n",
    "   `spatial_diagnostics_decision()`) on every fitted model,\n",
    "4. a **DGP-recovery stress test** that simulates each cross-sectional DGP and\n",
    "   checks whether the decision tree lands on the correct generating model, and\n",
    "5. short worked examples for **panel** and **spatial-flow** diagnostics.\n",
    "\n",
    "## Background\n",
    "\n",
    "The classical Anselin–Florax / Koley–Bera LM family tests:\n",
    "\n",
    "| Test | $H_0$ | Alternative | df |\n",
    "|------|-------|-------------|-----|\n",
    "| LM-Lag | $\\rho = 0$ | SAR | 1 |\n",
    "| LM-Error | $\\lambda = 0$ | SEM | 1 |\n",
    "| LM-WX | $\\gamma = 0$ | SLX | $k_{wx}$ |\n",
    "| LM-SDM (joint) | $\\rho = \\gamma = 0$ | SDM | $1 + k_{wx}$ |\n",
    "| LM-SLX-Error (joint) | $\\lambda = \\gamma = 0$ | SDEM | $1 + k_{wx}$ |\n",
    "| Robust LM-Lag | $\\rho = 0$ robust to $\\lambda$ | SAR vs SEM | 1 |\n",
    "| Robust LM-Error | $\\lambda = 0$ robust to $\\rho$ | SEM vs SAR | 1 |\n",
    "| Robust LM-Lag-SDM | $\\rho = 0$ robust to $\\gamma$ | SDM | 1 |\n",
    "| Robust LM-WX | $\\gamma = 0$ robust to $\\rho$ | SDM | $k_{wx}$ |\n",
    "| Robust LM-Error-SDEM | $\\lambda = 0$ robust to $\\gamma$ | SDEM | 1 |\n",
    "| LM-WX-SEM | $\\gamma = 0$ in SEM | SDEM | $k_{wx}$ |\n",
    "| LM-Error-SDM | $\\lambda = 0$ in SDM | SDARAR | 1 |\n",
    "| LM-Lag-SDEM | $\\rho = 0$ in SDEM | SDARAR | 1 |\n",
    "\n",
    "The Bayesian analogue evaluates the LM statistic at every posterior draw,\n",
    "yielding a **posterior distribution** of the LM statistic rather than a single\n",
    "point estimate.  Robust variants use the **Neyman orthogonal score** of\n",
    "Doğan, Taşpınar & Bera (2021) to remove correlation between the test score and\n",
    "the nuisance score:\n",
    "\n",
    "$$g_\\psi^* = g_\\psi - J_{\\psi\\phi \\cdot \\sigma}\\,J_{\\phi\\phi\\cdot\\sigma}^{-1}\\,g_\\phi.$$\n",
    "\n",
    "The same machinery extends to balanced panels (with a $T$ multiplier on the\n",
    "information matrix) and to origin–destination flow models built on Kronecker\n",
    "weight matrices $W_d, W_o, W_w$.  All three families are demonstrated below."
   ]

    "## 4. Robust Bayesian LM Tests (Neyman Orthogonal Score)\n",
    "\n",
    "These tests use the **Neyman orthogonal score adjustment** from Dogan et al. (2021, Proposition 3) to ensure robustness against local misspecification in the nuisance parameter. This is the key innovation over the classical Bera-Yoon (1993) approach.\n",
    "\n",
    "The adjustment removes the correlation between the test parameter score and the nuisance parameter score:\n",
    "\n",
    "$$g_\\psi^* = g_\\psi - J_{\\psi\\phi \\cdot \\sigma} \\, J_{\\phi\\phi \\cdot \\sigma}^{-1} \\, g_\\phi$$\n",
    "\n",
    "where $J_{\\cdot \\cdot \\cdot \\sigma}$ denotes information matrix blocks partitioned on $\\sigma^2$."
   ]
