    "## Conceptual Roadmap\n",
    "\n",
    "Let $W$ denote the spatial weights matrix and $WX$ the spatial lag of covariates.\n",
    "\n",
    "- **SLX**: $y = X\\beta + WX\\theta + \\epsilon$\n",
    "- **SAR**: $y = \\rho Wy + X\\beta + \\epsilon$\n",
    "- **SEM**: $y = X\\beta + u$, $u = \\lambda Wu + \\epsilon$\n",
    "- **SDM**: $y = \\rho Wy + X\\beta + WX\\theta + \\epsilon$\n",
    "- **SDEM**: $y = X\\beta + WX\\theta + u$, $u = \\lambda Wu + \\epsilon$\n",
    "\n",
    "In `bayespecon`, all models accept either:\n",
    "- formula mode: `model_class(formula=..., data=..., W=...)`, or\n",
    "- matrix mode: `model_class(y=..., X=..., W=...)`."

    "### MCMC Sampling Adequacy\n",
    "\n",
    "Bayesian estimation of spatial models has a well-known efficiency\n",
    "pitfall: the spatial dependence parameter $\\rho$ (or $\\lambda$) often\n",
    "mixes slowly, so a chain that *looks* converged in its point estimate\n",
    "can still produce posterior credible intervals that are 10–12 % too\n",
    "narrow because the sampler has not visited the tails enough times\n",
    "{cite:p}`wolf2018StochasticEfficiency`. The\n",
    "`spatial_mcmc_diagnostic` helper checks effective sample size, sampler\n",
    "yield, $\\hat{R}$, and HPDI stability for the spatial parameter and\n",
    "warns when any threshold is violated.\n"
   ]

    "### Bayes Factor Model Comparison\n",
    "\n",
    "Bayes factors provide an alternative to information criteria for comparing competing models. While WAIC and LOO assess *predictive* performance, Bayes factors compare *marginal likelihoods* — the probability of the data under each model, integrated over all parameter values weighted by the prior:\n",
    "\n",
    "$$BF_{ij} = \\frac{p(y \\mid \\mathcal{M}_i)}{p(y \\mid \\mathcal{M}_j)} = \\frac{ML_i}{ML_j}$$\n",
    "\n",
    "This makes Bayes factors sensitive to the *prior*: models with diffuse priors on unnecessary parameters are penalized more heavily, because the marginal likelihood averages over all possible parameter values weighted by the prior. In spatial models, this means that models with many WX coefficients under wide priors (e.g., SLX, SDM, SDEM) can receive substantially lower marginal likelihoods than more parsimonious alternatives (e.g., OLS, SAR, SEM) — even if their log-likelihoods are similar.\n",
    "\n",
    "**Interpreting Bayes factors** (Kass & Raftery, 1995):\n",
    "\n",
    "| BF range | Evidence strength |\n",
    "|---|---|\n",
    "| 1 – 3 | Anecdotal |\n",
    "| 3 – 10 | Moderate |\n",
    "| 10 – 30 | Strong |\n",
    "| 30 – 100 | Very strong |\n",
    "| > 100 | Extreme |\n",
    "\n",
    "**Method.** We use bridge sampling (Meng & Wong, 1996) to estimate each model's log marginal likelihood, following the R `bridgesampling` package (Gronau et al., 2020). The implementation uses ESS weighting, two-phase convergence, and MCSE diagnostics. For reliable estimates, 40,000+ posterior samples are recommended.\n",
    "\n",
    "**Important caveat.** Bayes factors can be very sensitive to prior specification. Models with diffuse priors on many parameters will tend to have lower marginal likelihoods. This is by design — it is the Bayesian Occam's razor at work — but it means that Bayes factors and information criteria may disagree when priors are uninformative."
   ]

    "#### Bridge Sampling vs. BIC\n",
    "\n",
    "- BIC: SAR > SLX > OLS > SEM (SAR wins, SLX is moderate, SEM is worst)\n",
    "- Bridge: SAR > SEM > OLS (SAR wins, SEM is moderate)\n",
    "\n",
    "The two methods can produce qualitatively different model rankings. This is expected and reflects a fundamental difference in what they assume about priors:\n",
    "\n",
    "- **BIC** approximates $\\log(ML) \\approx \\hat\\ell_{\\max} - \\frac{k}{2}\\log(n)$, which assumes *unit-information priors* (priors containing as much information as a single observation). The penalty per parameter is fixed at $\\frac{1}{2}\\log(n) \\approx 2.1$ for $n = 77$.\n",
    "\n",
    "- **Bridge sampling** integrates over the *actual* priors in the model. When priors are wide (e.g., `Normal(0, 100)` on WX coefficients), the marginal likelihood penalizes each such parameter by roughly $\\log(\\sigma_{\\text{prior}} / \\sigma_{\\text{post}})$, which can be 5–10× larger than the BIC penalty.\n",
    "\n",
    "This is why models with many WX terms (SLX, SDM, SDEM) may look reasonable under BIC but receive extreme Bayes factors under bridge sampling: the wide priors on the WX coefficients are \"wasted\" — they spread probability mass over implausible parameter values, reducing the marginal likelihood. This is **Bayesian Occam's razor** at work, and bridge sampling is generally more trustworthy because it accounts for the actual prior specification."
